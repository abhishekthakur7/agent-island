# Define the parity acceptance standard

## Question

What observable evidence and allowed deviations determine whether Agent Island meets or exceeds functional, interaction, and visual parity without copying Vibe Island's identity or assets?

## Comments

### Resolution — 2026-07-18

The [parity acceptance standard](../assets/parity-acceptance-standard.md)
defines parity as evidence-backed user-outcome and quality parity, rather than
pixel, asset, copy, or implementation parity. It requires a traceable,
capability-aware record and reproducible acceptance scenario for every
applicable inventory item, with functional, interaction, visual-quality, and
identity-boundary gates.

It permits only documented scope, capability, evidence-gap, material
improvement, and platform/security deviations; each must state its impact and
safe fallback. Unsupported or unsafe actions remain explicit and route to an
honest Jump Back rather than being fabricated. The standard makes misownership,
ambiguous action routing, false health, stale attention, destructive cleanup,
and scope expansion release-blocking failures. Visual parity requires live
human review of representative states and independently authored Agent Island
identity work. Final deviation approval remains with the human owner in the
final approval workflow.
