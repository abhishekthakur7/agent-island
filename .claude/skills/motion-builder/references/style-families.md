# Style Families — the reference library

Named visual systems that seed a motion sheet. Pick one, adapt it with brand
inputs, done — the interview shrinks to two questions. Each family defines:
surface, type, palette bias, component language, motion bias, SFX bias, stage
suitability, and extra NEGATIVE lines.

Families 2-6 are transcribed from the user's own reference material — treat
them as house styles, not suggestions.

Quick picker:

| Family | Feels like | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| paper-editorial | premium tech explainer on cream paper | AI/dev explainers, the FRMWRKD default |
| vox-collage | documentary cutout theater, one continuous stage | news-style explainers, history, geopolitics, money |
| zine-collage | punk print zine, tape + ransom letters | culture, hot takes, youth-coded topics |
| ink-sport-vector | flat vector energy, spray grain | sports, hype, versus pieces, countdowns |
| paint-over-photo | painted strokes over real photos | athletes/people features, event recaps |
| type-punch | full-bleed kinetic type, logo-led | brand spots, openers, manifestos, CTAs |
| flat-duotone | clean SaaS illustration world | product explainers, onboarding, B2B |
| glass-dark | dark glass UI panels, neon accents | software demos, data, night-mode tech |

| terminal-neon | phosphor CRT / code world | dev tools, hacking, CLI topics |

## 1. paper-editorial — the FRMWRKD house default

**Essence:** premium tech-editorial design board; warm cream paper meets dark
glass "image cards"; everything typeset on an invisible grid.
**Surface:** warm light cream paper (#F4EFE2-ish), subtle fiber texture — never
pure white. **Stage:** works locked (a desk-like paper field) or free.
**Type:** bold uppercase grotesk H1 (tight tracking); semi-bold uppercase H2;
neutral sans body; mono for code/numbers.
**Palette bias:** cream + charcoal base; electric blue / violet / cyan for
intelligence; green for automation; orange/red only for warnings.
**Components:** rounded image cards with label bars + soft paper shadows; pill
chips; numbered tiles (01/02/03); thin connector lines; token meters; dark code
cards with readable short lines.
**Motion bias:** slow push-ins, cards sliding into place, lines drawing on,
panels expanding, data particles along wires. Calm, precise, satisfying.
**SFX bias:** soft whooshes, paper slides, UI ticks, gentle hums.
**Negative additions:** `no childish cartoon mascots, no cyberpunk city clutter,
no plastic 3D people`.

## 2. vox-collage — documentary cutout theater (from the Vox-style workflow)

**Essence:** the news-explainer collage: ONE locked background stage, cutout
photos popping up in layers like paper puppets. Reads as a single continuous
shot — the background never cuts, only elements enter and leave.
**Layer model (load-bearing):** BACKGROUND = the locked stage (a muted map,
archival texture, color field — shared across the whole video). MIDGROUND =
black-and-white halftone cutouts of people/subjects with a rough white keyline
and an **offset red marker stroke** behind each cutout (the slight 3D pop).
FOREGROUND = structures, props, charts, big stat numbers in full color.
**Surface:** aged paper / muted archival field; grain and print texture.
**Type:** condensed bold headline caps; numbers get huge (statistics are
characters in this style); small annotation labels like margin notes.
**Palette bias:** desaturated stage + B/W halftone people + one hot accent
(red) for strokes, underlines, arrows; secondary mustard or blue.
**Components:** halftone cutouts, red offset strokes, torn-paper edges, big
counters ("$116"), map pins, underline swipes, archival photo cards.
**Motion bias:** spring pop-ups with slight overshoot, **staggered** entrances
(never simultaneous), cutouts sliding in from off-frame, counters ticking up,
red underline swiping beneath a key word. Camera: subtle slow drift only —
the stage's stillness IS the style.
**SFX bias:** paper pops, thwips, stamp hits, counter ticks, low newsroom hum.
**Stage:** ALWAYS locked — this family is the reason the stage-lock exists.
**Negative additions:** `no glossy 3D, no lens flares, no camera cuts within
the clip, no full-color midground portraits (midground stays halftone B/W)`.

## 3. zine-collage — punk print collage (from user ref: poster set)

**Essence:** maximal analog collage — cream paper and saturated ink fields
covered in taped-down photo cutouts, ransom-note lettering, marker scribbles.
Looks hand-assembled, mass-printed, slightly illegal.
**Surface:** cream stock AND saturated ink-blue/red poster fields; visible
print grain, halftone dots, misregistration.
**Type:** ransom-note mixed cutout letters for accents; bold condensed
headlines; hand-marker annotations in red; typewriter labels; mixed scripts
welcome (Latin + CJK energy).
**Palette bias:** cream, cobalt/royal blue, tomato red, black; occasional
process-color pops from stickers.
**Components:** photo cutouts with rough white keylines, masking-tape strips,
stickers, red hand-drawn circles and teardrops, starburst badges, halftone
photo patches, barcode/stamp ephemera.
**Motion bias:** tape slaps down, cutouts drop with a bounce, letters snap in
one-by-one, scribbles draw on, sticker peels. Stop-motion cadence (steppy,
8-12 fps feel) over buttery ease.
**SFX bias:** tape rips, paper crumples, stamp thunks, camera-shutter clicks.
**Stage:** free (each clip its own poster) or locked corkboard/table.
**Negative additions:** `no clean vector minimalism, no soft gradients, no
glossy UI, no perfect alignment — controlled mess is the style`.

## 4. ink-sport-vector — flat vector energy (from user ref: blue sports frames)

**Essence:** bold flat-vector illustration with spray-grain texture; faceless
figures mid-action; compositions built on hard diagonals and circular sweeps.
**Surface:** flat cobalt-blue and black fields; grain/spray texture in the
darks; no gradients beyond texture.
**Type:** heavy geometric sans, often on a curve or diagonal; white or yellow.
**Palette bias:** cobalt blue, black, white, tomato red, mustard yellow — hard
cuts between fields, no blends.
**Components:** faceless athletes/figures in silhouette-plus-fill, motion
trails as ribbon shapes, starburst impact shards, court/field diagrams from
above, oversized ball/object close-ups.
**Motion bias:** whip pans between fields of color, elements slamming in on
beat, ribbon trails sweeping through, camera orbiting a frozen action pose,
speed lines. Fast in, hard stop, hold.
**SFX bias:** whip whooshes, impact thuds, crowd swells (ambient, not music).
**Stage:** free — energy comes from changing angles.
**Negative additions:** `no photorealism, no facial features, no soft shadows,
no thin delicate linework`.

## 5. paint-over-photo — painted sports editorial (from user ref: player frame)

**Essence:** real photography with aggressive hand-painted energy on top —
brush-stroke bursts, painted lettering cutting across the subject, colors that
ignore the photo's palette. The photo is the anchor; the paint is the voice.
**Surface:** full-bleed photograph, often desaturated slightly so paint pops.
**Type:** hand-painted brush caps (imperfect, energetic), sometimes stencil;
words break across lines and overlap the subject.
**Palette bias:** photo-neutral base + electric yellow, green, red, cobalt
paint; white keylines around the hero subject.
**Components:** painted stroke bursts radiating from action points, drip
accents, keyline-traced subjects, painted underlines, real logos/badges kept
intact (fetch via logos.py — never let the model repaint a crest).
**Motion bias:** paint strokes splash on in 2-3 frames-worth steps, letters
write on stroke-by-stroke, photo does a slow punch-in while paint animates,
subject pops off a keyline. The PHOTO stays still or near-still; the PAINT moves.
**SFX bias:** brush swipes, splat hits, marker squeaks, stadium air.
**Stage:** free; per-clip photo. Strong v2v family — paint over the user's
own footage (v2v-overlay).
**Negative additions:** `no digital glow effects, no lens flares, no 3D text,
no clean geometric shapes — everything added must read as paint`.

## 6. type-punch — full-bleed kinetic type (from user ref: RUN frames)

**Essence:** the message IS the visual: screen-filling ultra-bold type over
textured color fields or blurred footage; logo-led; three words per clip max.
**Surface:** saturated solid fields (red/black/white) or heavily blurred/
duotoned footage behind the type.
**Type:** ultra-bold grotesk, tight tracking, screen-edge-to-edge; words
stacked; occasional italic slam for the key word.
**Palette bias:** two or three colors TOTAL per video (e.g. red/black/white).
Discipline is the style.
**Components:** giant words, a single logo mark, thin rule lines, duotone
footage plates behind type.
**Motion bias:** hard cuts between word states, type scaling one notch per
beat, background footage drifting slowly under static type, flash inversions
(dark↔light), the logo appearing once, dead center, holding.
**SFX bias:** deep thuds on word changes, air risers, room tone. Rhythm
matters more than variety.
**Stage:** locked palette + type system; backgrounds may vary within it.
**Negative additions:** `no decorative fonts, no more than 4 words on screen,
no busy backgrounds fighting the type, no drop shadows`.

## 7. flat-duotone — clean SaaS illustration

**Essence:** friendly flat-line illustration world: white field, gray line
architecture, characters and objects in exactly two accent colors.
**Surface:** white/off-white; faint gray cityscape/room linework as context.
**Type:** clean geometric sans, sentence case, modest sizes.
**Palette bias:** white + warm gray lines + two accents (e.g. mustard +
magenta). Accents mean "look here"; gray means context.
**Components:** line-drawn people with flat accent clothing, simple props,
floating panels/cards, coin/asset icons, dotted paths between elements.
**Motion bias:** characters walk/gesture in loops, paths draw on, panels float
up gently, scenes slide laterally to the next vignette (feels like one long
mural — pairs well with a locked stage).
**SFX bias:** soft pops, light ticks, airy transitions.
**Negative additions:** `no gradients, no 3D, no photo textures, no faces with
detailed features, no more than two accent colors`.

## 8. glass-dark — dark glass UI panels

**Essence:** floating translucent dark-glass panels in a black void; neon rim
light; the "serious software at night" look. (The dark-card half of the user's
AI-agents pack, promoted to a standalone family.)
**Surface:** near-black void with subtle depth fog; glass panels with 1px
light borders. **Type:** clean UI sans; mono for data.
**Palette bias:** charcoal/black + violet, cyan, electric blue glows; green
for success states.
**Components:** glass cards, node graphs with glowing wires, terminals,
status pills, holographic charts.
**Motion bias:** panels drifting in parallax, wires lighting up directionally,
UI expanding from a point, camera slow-orbiting a graph.
**SFX bias:** synthetic ticks, data chirps, low room hum.
**Negative additions:** `no daylight scenes, no paper textures, no cartoon
characters, no overexposed bloom`.

## 9. blueprint — technical drawing

**Essence:** engineering blueprint / patent-drawing world: grid paper, ruled
construction lines, annotated diagrams that draw themselves.
**Surface:** deep blueprint blue with white lines, or cream drafting paper
with graphite. **Type:** technical lettering caps, dimension labels, stamps.
**Components:** exploded-view diagrams, dimension arrows, dashed construction
lines, circled detail callouts, revision stamps.
**Motion bias:** lines drafting on stroke-by-stroke, exploded parts assembling,
camera tracking along a schematic, detail circles zooming.
**SFX bias:** pencil scratches, ruler slides, paper rolls, stamp thunks.
**Negative additions:** `no color fills beyond the two-tone scheme, no
photorealism, no glow effects`.

## 10. terminal-neon — CRT code world

**Essence:** phosphor-green/amber terminals, scanlines, ASCII structure; the
machine's point of view.
**Components:** terminal windows, blinking cursors, ASCII diagrams, log
streams, pixel progress bars. **Motion bias:** type-on text, scrolling logs,
glitch-free wipes, cursor leading the camera. **SFX bias:** key clacks, relay
clicks, CRT hum. **Negative additions:** `no modern flat UI, no photoreal
hands, no excessive glitch distortion (readability first)`.

## 11. soft-3d — matte clay renders

**Essence:** friendly matte-clay 3D objects on seamless studio backdrops;
soft global illumination; metaphors as toys.
**Components:** rounded clay objects, pastel seamless sweeps, simple
character-less hands/props, floating shapes. **Motion bias:** squash-and-
stretch pops, objects assembling from parts, turntable rotations, gentle
physics drops. **SFX bias:** soft boops, felt taps, marimba-adjacent ticks
(percussive, not musical). **Negative additions:** `no photorealism, no hard
speculars, no busy scenes — max 3 hero objects per frame`.

## Adapting a family

Swap palette to brand colors, swap the H1 voice, keep the component language
and motion bias intact — that's what makes it read as the family. If a request
fights the family's physics (glassy reflections in vox-collage), flag it: mixed
families are the #1 cause of "AI-ish" output. One family per sheet, always.
